My First 100 Days

Building the Bridge to Deliver the 2025-28 Strategic Plan

Head of Corporate Services - Shafquat Ahmed

My Core Philosophy

The Strategic Plan is our destination.
My role is to build the bridge that gets us there.

This bridge rests on two essential pillars:

  • Operational Excellence: Robust, compliant systems that ensure stability and maximise social impact.
  • Deep, Trusting Relationships: Collaborative leadership that earns confidence across all levels - colleagues, peers, and Board.

The 100-Day Roadmap

Three focused phases with clear outcomes:

PHASE 1: Days 1-30

Deep Immersion

Understanding OPFS's culture, operational realities, and strategic context through direct engagement with colleagues, services, and stakeholders.

PHASE 2: Days 31-70

Strategic Framework Development

Co-creating the Corporate Strategy Framework with the senior leadership team, addressing resource allocation, funding models, and governance structures.

PHASE 3: Days 71-100

Implementation & Performance Framework

Establishing performance management frameworks, socialising plans, and creating sustainable review processes.

Phase 1: Strategic Immersion (Days 1-30)

Understanding Our Mission in Practice

  • Shadow frontline services across Scotland's diverse communities - from helplines to local authority partnerships
  • Analyse financial sustainability - mapping and evaluating our entire funding ecosystem - from public sector and lottery funding to trusts, individual donations, corporate partnerships, and earned income. This will help build a diversified and resilient financial model
  • Review governance structures - ensuring Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator (OSCR) compliance and Board effectiveness
  • Assess operational capacity - understanding colleague workloads, systems, and resource constraints
Key Deliverable: Comprehensive situational analysis with initial recommendations for the senior leadership team.

Phase 2: Framework Development (Days 31-70)

Building Our Strategic Foundation

  • Facilitate strategic workshops with the senior leadership team to address critical questions:
    • Resource prioritisation across Scotland's 32 local authorities
    • Optimal balance of restricted vs. unrestricted funding
    • Service delivery models that maximise impact
  • Develop risk management protocols addressing funding, safeguarding, and operational risks
  • Design performance management framework using Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), Most Significant Change (MSC) methodology, and Social Return on Investment (SROI) analysis to measure our social impact
Key Deliverable: Draft Corporate Strategy Framework ready for Board consultation.

Phase 3: Systems & Culture (Days 71-100)

Embedding Excellence

  • Implement Performance Management Framework to measure poverty reduction impact and wellbeing outcomes
  • Establish quarterly Strategy Reviews for senior leadership team with clear progress tracking against our corporate objectives
  • Create Board dashboard providing a concise, one-page overview of organisational health across three critical areas: financial sustainability, risk, and impact metrics
  • Launch communication protocols ensuring transparency and no-surprises approach across the organisation
Key Deliverable: Fully operational performance and governance frameworks with documented processes.

Building Trust Through Action

Trust is earned through transparent communication, consistent delivery, and leadership that demonstrates genuine care for people and mission.

With Colleagues

  • Monthly check-ins across all locations, recognising hybrid working realities
  • Process simplification to reduce administrative burden
  • Workload advocacy ensuring sustainable, empathetic service delivery

With Senior Leadership Team

  • Weekly 1-on-1s focused on enabling their success
  • Transparent communication about challenges and opportunities
  • Collaborative decision-making that strengthens team cohesion

With the Board

  • Clear reporting on strategic progress and compliance
  • Proactive risk management with mitigation plans
  • Strategic insights that inform governance decisions

My Commitment to OPFS

By Day 100, OPFS will have:

  • A robust Corporate Strategy Framework co-created with the leadership team
  • Clear Performance Management Framework tracking real-world impact
  • Strengthened governance and risk management processes
  • A culture of transparency and collaboration at all levels
My ultimate goal: To ensure our corporate services provide the strongest possible foundation for the life-changing work you do - challenging poverty and transforming lives across Scotland.

I am ready to build this bridge together.
